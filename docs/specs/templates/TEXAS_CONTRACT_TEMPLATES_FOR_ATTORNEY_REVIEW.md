## 1. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

### Title
**EN**: INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT
**ES**: ACUERDO DE CONTRATISTA INDEPENDIENTE

### Critical attorney decision

`[ATTORNEY REVIEW]`: Verify driver classification. IRS 20-factor test, DOL Final Rule on Independent Contractor Status (effective March 11, 2024), and Texas Workforce Commission factors. If drivers cannot meet ALL elements of the independent contractor test as applied by the U.S. Department of Labor, this MUST be an Employee Driver Agreement instead. Misclassification creates back-wages liability + tax liability + penalties.

For B1 visa drivers specifically, additional considerations apply.

## 3. DOT DRUG & ALCOHOL TESTING CONSENT

### Title
**EN**: DOT DRUG & ALCOHOL TESTING CONSENT AND NOTIFICATION
**ES**: CONSENTIMIENTO Y NOTIFICACIÓN DE PRUEBAS DE DROGAS Y ALCOHOL DEL DOT

### Required by
49 CFR Part 382 (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations — Controlled Substances and Alcohol Use and Testing)

### Draft text

I, **`[VARIABLE: driver_full_legal_name]`**, in connection with my providing commercial motor vehicle driving services to **`[VARIABLE: operating_company_legal_name]`** (the "Company"), hereby acknowledge and consent to the following:

#### 1. NOTIFICATION OF DOT TESTING PROGRAM

I have been notified that the Company is subject to U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) drug and alcohol testing rules under 49 CFR Part 382, which require commercial motor vehicle drivers to be tested in the following circumstances:

(a) **Pre-employment testing** — Before performing any safety-sensitive function (including operating a commercial motor vehicle)

(b) **Reasonable suspicion testing** — When a trained supervisor has reasonable suspicion based on specific, contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning my appearance, behavior, speech, or body odors

(c) **Random testing** — On an unannounced basis at the minimum rates required by DOT (currently 50% for controlled substances and 10% for alcohol, or such other rates as DOT may set)

(d) **Post-accident testing** — Following accidents meeting DOT criteria under 49 CFR 382.303

(e) **Return-to-duty testing** — Before returning to safety-sensitive duties following a positive test or refusal

(f) **Follow-up testing** — As directed by a Substance Abuse Professional following a positive test or refusal

#### 2. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES TESTED

I have been notified that DOT testing includes the following substances at minimum:
- Marijuana metabolites (THC)
- Cocaine metabolites
- Amphetamines (including methamphetamine and MDMA)
- Opioids (including codeine, morphine, heroin, hydrocodone, hydromorphone, oxycodone, oxymorphone)
- Phencyclidine (PCP)

The Company may, in addition, conduct non-DOT testing for additional substances permitted under Texas law.

#### 3. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATIONS

I understand that a verified positive test result, a refusal to test, or other prohibited conduct under 49 CFR 382.211–.217 will result in:

(a) Immediate removal from safety-sensitive duties
(b) Reporting to the FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse
(c) Requirement to complete the DOT return-to-duty process before resuming driving
(d) Termination of my engagement with the Company, in the Company's discretion
(e) Reporting to my state CDL-issuing agency as required by law

#### 4. REFUSAL TO TEST

I understand that the following constitute a refusal to test under DOT rules:

(a) Failing to appear for any test within a reasonable time
(b) Failing to remain at the testing site until the testing process is complete
(c) Failing to provide a specimen
(d) Failing to permit observation when directly observed collection is required
(e) Tampering with or attempting to adulterate a specimen
(f) Admitting adulteration or substitution to the Medical Review Officer

Refusal to test has the same consequences as a verified positive test.

#### 5. SAP / RETURN-TO-DUTY

I understand that following a positive test or refusal, I must complete the DOT return-to-duty process with a qualified Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) at my own expense, including:

(a) SAP evaluation
(b) SAP-prescribed treatment or education program
(c) Follow-up SAP evaluation
(d) Negative return-to-duty test
(e) Follow-up testing program (minimum 6 tests in first 12 months)

#### 6. CLEARINGHOUSE QUERIES

I authorize the Company to query the FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse for my records:

(a) Pre-employment full query
(b) Annual limited query during employment

I have separately executed a Clearinghouse consent if required by FMCSA rule.

#### 7. CONSENT TO TESTING

I consent to:

(a) Provide urine, breath, oral fluid, or hair specimens (as DOT rules permit) for testing
(b) Be directly observed during collection where DOT rules require
(c) Release of test results from the Medical Review Officer to the Company's Designated Employer Representative
(d) Reporting of violations to the Clearinghouse and to my state CDL agency

#### 8. POLICY ACKNOWLEDGMENT

I have received a copy of the Company's written Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy dated `[VARIABLE: policy_date]`, and I have had the opportunity to ask questions. I understand it is my responsibility to know and comply with the Policy.

This Consent is executed in `[VARIABLE: primary_language]`. A `[VARIABLE: secondary_language]` translation has been provided. I have read and understand this Consent in `[VARIABLE: primary_language]`.

### Purpose

### Draft sections

#### 1. EQUIPMENT ASSIGNMENT

The Company assigns to the Driver, for use in performing transportation services, the following equipment (the "Equipment"):

- Tractor unit number: `[VARIABLE: unit_number]`
- VIN: `[VARIABLE: vin]`

- Trailer (if applicable): `[VARIABLE: trailer_number]`

The Equipment is provided for the sole purpose of performing Company-dispatched transportation services and authorized personal conveyance under DOT rules.

#### 2. PRE-USE INSPECTION

Before first use, the Driver and a Company representative shall jointly inspect the Equipment and document its condition with photographs and a written checklist. The Driver acknowledges the Equipment is delivered in the condition documented.

The Driver shall perform pre-trip and post-trip inspections in accordance with 49 CFR 392.7 and 396.11.

#### 3. AUTHORIZED USE

The Driver shall use the Equipment only:

(a) For Company-dispatched loads
(b) For deadhead movement to/from dispatched loads
(c) For authorized personal conveyance under DOT rules and Company policy
(d) For necessary repair and maintenance trips
(e) For training and orientation as directed by the Company

The Driver shall NOT use the Equipment for:

(a) Personal travel unrelated to authorized personal conveyance
(b) Carrying unauthorized passengers (including family members) without prior written Company approval
(c) Transportation of unauthorized cargo
(d) Operations outside scope of valid CDL
(e) Operations while medically disqualified

#### 4. DAMAGE LIABILITY

**4.1 Driver Responsibility**: The Driver is responsible for damage to Equipment caused by:

(a) The Driver's negligence (e.g., backing accidents, failure to inspect, failure to follow maintenance schedule)
(b) Driver's willful misconduct
(c) Violation of Company written policy (e.g., unauthorized off-road use)
(d) Operation while impaired or in violation of hours-of-service rules

**4.2 NOT Driver Responsibility**: The Driver is NOT responsible for damage from:

(a) Normal wear and tear
(b) Manufacturer defects
(c) Acts of third parties not within Driver's control
(d) Acts of nature (weather, road conditions properly accommodated)
(e) Mechanical failure that occurs despite proper inspection and maintenance

**4.3 Damage Calculation**: Where Driver responsibility is established, the deduction amount shall be the LESSER of:

(a) Actual repair cost (with documentation)
(b) Fair market depreciated value of the damaged component

The Company shall provide the Driver with written documentation including:
- Photographs of damage
- Repair estimates from qualified vendors

## 5. EMPLOYEE NON-COMPETE / NON-SOLICITATION (TEXAS BCC §15.50)

### Critical attorney input

`[ATTORNEY REVIEW — CRITICAL]`: Texas non-competes are governed by Texas Business and Commerce Code §15.50–.52 and are highly fact-specific. To be enforceable, a non-compete must:

1. Be **ancillary to an otherwise enforceable agreement** at the time it is made (typically the employment agreement);
2. Contain **reasonable limits as to time, geographical area, and scope of activity** to be restrained;
3. Impose limitations that do not impose a **greater restraint than necessary** to protect the company's goodwill or other legitimate business interest;
4. Be supported by **consideration** beyond mere at-will employment (typically: confidential information access, specialized training, equity, signing bonus).

Recent Texas Supreme Court cases (Marsh USA Inc. v. Cook 2011; Exxon Mobil v. Drennen 2014) emphasize "ancillary to" requirement. The FTC's now-blocked non-compete ban (2024) is on hold but signals federal scrutiny.

**For drivers**: Non-competes for ordinary drivers are unlikely to be enforceable absent unique circumstances. Recommend not using.

**For office employees**: May be enforceable for those with access to confidential customer information, pricing, broker relationships, or trade secrets.

### Draft (employee version)

- Confidential customer lists, customer rates, and customer payment histories
- Confidential broker contacts and load tendering procedures
- Confidential operational procedures, training materials, and business strategies
- Specialized training paid for by the Company

WHEREAS, in consideration of: (a) the Company's promise to provide such confidential information, (b) `[VARIABLE: consideration_signing_bonus_amount]` signing consideration, and (c) continued employment;

THE EMPLOYEE AGREES TO THE FOLLOWING RESTRICTIONS:

#### 2. NON-COMPETE

(a) Engage in or own any interest in (other than passive investment of less than 5% of a publicly traded company) any motor carrier or freight brokerage business

(b) Within the geographic area of: **the State of Texas plus any state in which the Employee performed material business activities for the Company during the last twelve (12) months of employment**

#### 3. NON-SOLICITATION OF CUSTOMERS

For a period of **eighteen (18) months** following termination, the Employee shall NOT, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit transportation or brokerage business from any Company customer with whom the Employee had material contact during the last twelve (12) months of employment

(b) "Material contact" means any customer for whom the Employee booked loads, negotiated rates, or had personal communication on more than three (3) occasions

#### 4. NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES

(b) Encourage any current Company employee or contractor driver to terminate their relationship with the Company

#### 5. REASONABLENESS ACKNOWLEDGMENT

(a) The restrictions in Sections 2, 3, and 4 are reasonable in time, geography, and scope

This blue-penciling authorization is itself part of the consideration.

#### 7. REMEDIES

(a) **Injunctive relief**: Money damages would be inadequate. The Company is entitled to seek injunctive relief without posting bond.

(b) **Damages**: In addition to injunctive relief, the Company may recover actual damages, lost profits, and attorney's fees as permitted by Texas Business and Commerce Code §15.51.

(b) This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Texas.
