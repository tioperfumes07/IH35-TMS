# Day-by-Day Runbook (May 16-21)

## Purpose
High-level cutover week runbook: who does what, when to escalate, and daily rhythm.

## Key roles
- Dispatch lead
- Accounting lead
- Driver manager
- Safety lead
- Owner/on-call approver

## May 16 - Prep day
- Confirm trainer assignments for office and drivers
- Confirm all draft docs are accessible
- Assign screenshot owner for each office guide
- Publish contact list and escalation tree

## May 17 - Office training wave 1
- Train Dispatch board basics and Booking a load
- Train managing Customers and Drivers
- Capture open questions and ownership

## May 18 - Office training wave 2
- Train Settlement workflow and Dispute resolution
- Train Accounting basics and keyboard shortcuts
- Confirm escalation paths are understood

## May 19 - Driver training day
- Roll out EN/ES install and usage guides
- Verify each active driver can log in
- Practice viewing loads + signing BOL + photo upload
- Practice issue escalation flow

## May 20 - Readiness checkpoint
- Run cross-functional go/no-go huddle
- Review unresolved risks and owners
- Confirm communication channels for cutover day

## May 21 - Go-live support rhythm
- Start-of-shift readiness check
- Midday blocker review
- End-of-day incident debrief and next actions

## Escalation paths
- Dispatch blocker -> Dispatch lead -> Owner
- Accounting/settlement blocker -> Accounting lead -> Owner
- Driver app blocker -> Driver manager -> Dispatch lead/support
- Safety/compliance blocker -> Safety lead -> Owner (immediate for high risk)

## Daily communication cadence
- Morning huddle: priorities and risk scan
- Midday pulse: blockers and ETAs
- End-of-day closeout: what happened, what is next

## Final note
This runbook is draft guidance for cutover prep and support. Final training package requires screenshot replacement and owner approval.
